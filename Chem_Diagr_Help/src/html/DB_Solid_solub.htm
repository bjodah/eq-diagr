Solubility of solids:
Dissolution or precipitation?

The solubility of oxides, hydroxides or oxyhydroxides for many transition metals is strongly dependent on the particle size and crystallinity of the solid.

For Fe(III), Al(III), etc, there is a large difference between the solubilities of crystalline oxides and amorphous hydroxides. The amorphous solids precipitate when the oversaturation is high, as when mixing solutions in the laboratory. The amorphous solids will slowly re-crystallise and form more insoluble (more stable) solids. See for example:

D Langmuir, Aqueous Environmental Geochemistry. Prentice-Hall, Inc., 1997. Sect.7.7 and Chapter 12, diagrams on p. 255 & 438.

The following diagram shows that for Fe(III) the difference is a factor of almost 105:

The endings “(am)” and “(cr)” in the formulas are phase indicators. The diagram shows that [Fe(III)]TOT ≈ 10−12.3 when haematite (Fe2O3) is in equilibrium with an aqueous solution of pH = 8. The diagram also shows that you can have a 10−5 mM solution of Fe(III) at pH 2 without precipitation: the solution is metastable. If the pH is increased, amorphous Fe(OH)3 precipitates at pH about 4.3

Modelling dissolution or precipitation?

To model the precipitation of Fe(III), the diagram above shows that the amorphous hydroxide, Fe(OH)3(am), should be included in the calcualtions, but the crystalline phases should be excluded (Fe2O3(cr) and FeOOH(cr)). On the other hand, to model a dissolution reaction the dissolving solid must be included in the calculations.

For advanced users:
Search preferences for solids in DATABASE:

If you edit the ini-file .DataBase.ini with a text editor, and change the entry All_Solids_Ask to true (or set advancedMenu = true), and then re-start DATABASE, the menu “Options / Preferences / Solids” will be available. Selecting this menu option will show a secondary window where you can choose between:

  • Include all solids when searching the databases. This option is the default. This should be choosen to model processes where solids are added and their dissolution process is being studied.
  • Exclude solid names ending with “(cr)”. This option should be chosen when modelling precipitation processes.

Exclude solids from the calculations

To exclude solids, they can be deactivated in the calculations, see deactivate spcies in SPANA. Alternatively, you can exclude “(cr)”-solids from the search in DATABASE.
